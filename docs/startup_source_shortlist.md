# Startup Source Shortlist

As of April 8, 2026, the strongest public startup discovery sources to build against
first are:

## 1. Y Combinator Startup Directory

- Los Angeles startups: https://www.ycombinator.com/companies/location/los-angeles
- SF Bay Area startups currently hiring: https://www.ycombinator.com/companies/location/san-francisco-bay-area/hiring
- YC jobs hub: https://www.ycombinator.com/jobs

Why this is first:

- official source
- current and refreshed in 2026
- high startup quality
- easy to segment by geography and hiring status
- naturally connects to founder and operator outreach

## 2. Built In local startup hubs

- Los Angeles: https://www.builtinla.com/
- San Francisco: https://www.builtinsf.com/

Why this is next:

- good for broader local ecosystem coverage beyond YC
- often has company profiles, category pages, and hiring-focused editorial lists
- useful for LA and SF targeting where we care about local summer options

## 3. YC company detail pages

Example:

- https://www.ycombinator.com/companies/mount

Why this matters:

- richer detail than the listing pages
- often includes website, founder names, location, and jobs
- a good second-pass enrichment target once the directory importer is working

## Next after that

After the YC adapter is stable, the next source adapters I would prioritize are:

- Built In company hubs
- accelerator and incubator portfolio pages
- USC research center and faculty directories
- hacker house and residency program lists
