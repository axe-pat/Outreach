# HR-Tech / Recruiting-Tech Employer Ranking for a PM Candidate (2026)

*Prepared for Akshat (USC Marshall MBA; PM at FlairX on "Ziva," an AI video-interview product). Current as of July 2026. This is personal career/recruiting research, not investment advice.*

## TL;DR
- The strongest overall PM career bets in this landscape are **Ashby** (fastest-growing modern ATS — 135% YoY revenue growth, sub-1x burn multiple, elite investors) and **HeyGen** ($100M ARR AI-video rocket, profitable since Q2 2023) — both pair top-tier momentum with prestige and strong comp; **Greenhouse**, **Mercor**, and **Tavus** round out the top tier depending on your risk appetite.
- Avoid or heavily scrutinize employers with repeated layoffs and stalled funding — **Beamery** (12% cut Jan 2023 then 25% Nov 2023 toward a 35% cost reduction, harsh Glassdoor reviews), **SeekOut** (7% cut Oct 2023 then ~30% May 2024 amid "$2 to earn $1" cash burn), and **Gem** (two layoffs, no new round since 2021) carry the clearest red flags.
- For Akshat specifically, the "compete" AI-interviewing category is small and early — Apriora/Alex, Ribbon, Sapia, ConverzAI are seed/Series A with thin PM comp data; the smarter career move may be to leverage your AI-interviewing domain expertise into adjacent, better-capitalized platforms (Ashby, Greenhouse, HeyGen, Tavus, Eightfold) rather than a lateral move to a same-stage rival.

## Key Findings
- **Capital is flooding AI-native recruiting infrastructure, not legacy tools.** Ashby announced a $50M Series D on July 22, 2025 (led by Mark McLaughlin at Alkeon, co-led by Lachy Groom, with F-Prime, Elad Gil, Gaingels) at 2x its Series C valuation; per founder Benji Encz, Ashby "scaled from 1,300 to over 2,700 customers, grown revenue by 135% year-over-year... a burn multiple of under 1x." Mercor raised $350M at a $10B valuation in October 2025 (5x step-up in eight months) and by July 2026 was reportedly in talks at a $20B valuation. HeyGen hit $100M ARR in October 2025, just 31 months after first reaching $1M. Meanwhile legacy/CRM incumbents (Gem, Beamery, SeekOut) have suffered layoffs and flat valuations.
- **PM compensation for MBA-level candidates in this space clusters at roughly $150K–$210K base / ~$180K–$260K total comp**, with AI-forward SF/NY companies at the top and PE-owned or non-coastal firms lower. Workday — the category's public anchor — pays a P4 (senior) PM a median ~$267K–$277K total and a P1 APM ~$143K (Levels.fyi).
- **Consolidation is accelerating and changes the "employer" calculus:** Workday acquired Paradox (closed Oct 1, 2025); SAP acquired SmartRecruiters (2025); Lever folded into Employ Inc.; iCIMS is PE-owned (Vista + TA Associates); Greenhouse, HireVue, and Bullhorn are all PE-owned. Several names on the base list are no longer independent.
- **The AI-interviewing battleground (Ziva's peers) is early and fragmented:** Apriora/Alex (~$19.8M raised, YC), Ribbon (~$8.2M seed, Radical Ventures), Sapia (~$24M), ConverzAI ($16M Series A, Menlo Ventures), with HireVue the mature but PE-owned (Carlyle) incumbent.

## Overall Synthesis: Top PM Bets Across All Categories

1. **Ashby** — The single best all-around bet. Fastest-growing modern ATS (135% YoY revenue growth, sub-1x burn), elite investors (Alkeon, F-Prime, Elad Gil, Lachy Groom), marquee AI-native customers (OpenAI, Ramp, Notion), and small enough (~193 people) that PMs get large scope. The premium brand in HR-tech VC circles right now.
2. **HeyGen** — Best pure trajectory. $100M ARR, profitable since Q2 2023, ~100K+ paying business customers, and transparent, strong PM comp ($160K–$210K base). Directly relevant to Ziva's avatar/video layer — domain-adjacent for Akshat.
3. **Greenhouse** — Best prestige + stability blend. #1 ATS brand recognition, ~$200M+ ARR, strong alumni network, likely IPO/exit candidate. Good resume equity even if slower-growth than Ashby.
4. **Mercor** — Highest-risk/highest-reward. $10B valuation (Oct 2025), reportedly in talks at $20B by July 2026, explosive growth — but concentrated business model, active Scale AI litigation, and a pivot away from pure recruiting. Elite signaling if you can get in.
5. **Tavus** — Best deep-tech bet. Sequoia/CRV-backed, research-driven, small team = high ownership; conversational-video expertise transfers directly from Ziva.
6. **Eightfold.ai** — Best enterprise-AI brand. Strongest resume signal in talent intelligence, launched its own AI Interviewer (direct Ziva-adjacent), well-capitalized ($2.1B) — though valuation has been flat since 2021.
7. **Workday** — Best comp + stability floor. Blue-chip prestige and the highest guaranteed comp (P4 ~$267K–$277K), and now owns Paradox, making it a growing force in AI recruiting.
8. **Synthesia** *(worth adding to your list)* — Best enterprise video-infra bet. $4B valuation, ~$146M ARR, GV/Nvidia-backed; larger and more structured than HeyGen, with strong Fortune 100 penetration.

## Recommendations
- **Stage 1 (now → next 3 months):** Prioritize **Ashby, HeyGen, Greenhouse, and Eightfold**. They combine growth, prestige, and PM scope, and each values AI-interviewing/video domain knowledge — use your Ziva/FlairX experience as the wedge in cover letters and interviews (e.g., "shipped an AI conversational-interview product competing with HireVue/Apriora").
- **Stage 2 (parallel swings):** For higher-upside/higher-risk, pursue **Mercor and Tavus**; for a stability floor and a comp anchor, keep **Workday** and **Bullhorn** in the funnel. Add **Synthesia** to your target list.
- **Stage 3 (compete-adjacent):** If you want to stay in AI-interviewing specifically, target **HireVue** (mature, stable, directly relevant) and **Apriora/Alex** (hot early-stage) — but weigh equity/dilution risk carefully at seed/Series A peers, and treat their PM comp as equity-weighted and cash-light.
- **Benchmarks that would change this ranking:** A new priced-up round or ARR milestone from Ashby/HeyGen/Synthesia reinforces their top ranking; any layoff or down-round at Eightfold or a collapse in Mercor's Scale AI litigation would trigger a downgrade; confirmation of a Greenhouse IPO would be an upgrade. Re-check **layoffs.fyi** and **Crunchbase** quarterly — this space moves fast.

## Caveats
- **PM comp data is thin and noisy** for most private startups here. The most reliable US PM total-comp figures are for HireVue (~$151K, Glassdoor), Paradox (~$150K, Glassdoor pre-acquisition), Eightfold (Sr PM ~$160K base, H1B database), and HeyGen ($160K–$210K posted base). Workday is the only fully transparent anchor (Levels.fyi). Treat all others as estimates; searches for "Gem PM" and "Mercor PM" are polluted by Gemini (crypto) and Project-Manager (not Product) listings respectively.
- **Several base-list companies are no longer independent:** Paradox (→Workday, Oct 2025), SmartRecruiters (→SAP, 2025), Lever (→Employ Inc.). iCIMS, Greenhouse, HireVue, and Bullhorn are PE-owned — meaning limited equity upside relative to a venture-stage startup.
- **Mercor's category fit is debatable** — it has largely pivoted from recruiting-tech to AI data-labeling/talent-for-AI-training, and one analyst publicly flagged its $10B valuation as potentially inflated (the same lead investor, Felicis, set both the Series B and C prices).
- **HackerRank revenue figures conflict** materially across trackers and should not be cited with confidence.
- **Suggested additions to your base list:** **Synthesia** (avatar/video infra — arguably the category leader by revenue) and, if broadening sourcing, **Mercor** (already discussed). **ProctorU** is only tangentially in recruiting (exam proctoring) and could be dropped.
- This is **personal career-research, not investment advice.** Funding and layoff news moves quickly; verify each company's current status immediately before interviewing.
